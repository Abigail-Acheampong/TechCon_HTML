History of TechCon

TechCon began in 1996 in New Orleans, Louisiana, as a modest gathering of tech enthusiasts and industry professionals. Over the years, it has grown into a premier global conference, hosted in various cities across the United States and beyond. The conference has evolved to address the rapid advancements in technology, from the early days of the internet to the current era of artificial intelligence and semiconductor innovation. Each year, TechCon attracts hundreds of attendees from diverse sectors, including utilities, semiconductors, and software development, fostering collaboration and innovation. Notable milestones include the introduction of CareerConnections in 2010, a recruiting event connecting students with industry leaders, and the expansion to include international delegates from over 11 countries by 2024.

Our Mission

TechCon's mission is to serve as a catalyst for technological innovation by convening industry leaders, researchers, and students to share knowledge and drive progress. We aim to foster a collaborative environment that advances cutting-edge solutions in fields like semiconductors, artificial intelligence, and energy infrastructure. By providing a platform for networking, education, and innovation, TechCon empowers attendees to shape the future of technology while addressing global challenges such as sustainability and digital transformation.

Notable Past Speakers

Deirdre Hanford

Deirdre Hanford, CEO of Natcast, spoke at TechCon 2024 about the future of semiconductor research. As the leader of the National Semiconductor Technology Center (NSTC), she emphasized the importance of public-private partnerships in advancing U.S. technological leadership. Hanford, a recipient of the 2025 IEEE Frederik Philips Award, has been recognized for her contributions to electronic design automation and energy-efficient microelectronics. Her insights at TechCon inspired attendees to explore innovative solutions in the semiconductor industry.
